Movement with Ocean Currents

Jellyfish are weak swimmers, relying mostly on ocean currents to move them around. They can contract and relax their bell for limited movement, but currents help them explore diverse habitats and find food more efficiently.

Stinging Tentacles

Jellyfish have tentacles covered in stinging cells (cnidocytes). These cells release venom to capture prey or deter predators. This ability is crucial for their defense and hunting smaller organisms like fish and plankton.

Transparent Body for Camouflage

The jellyfish gelatinous and transparent body helps it blend seamlessly into its surroundings. This natural camouflage reduces its visibility to predators in the open ocean.

Bioluminescence

Some jellyfish species can produce light through chemical reactions in their bodies. This bioluminescence helps them deter predators, attract prey, or communicate with other jellyfish in the dark depths of the ocean.

Reproductive Versatility

Jellyfish reproduce in two ways: sexually as medusas and asexually as polyps. This dual strategy allows them to multiply quickly and adapt to varying environmental conditions.
